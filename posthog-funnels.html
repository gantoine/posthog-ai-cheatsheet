Funnels Cheatsheet

Connective tissue between every other PostHog product

Why use it

Funnels are the connective tissue between every other PostHog product. Build a funnel for a key flow, click the drop-off step, jump straight into the replays of users who didn't convert.

Funnels tell you that something changed. Replay tells you why.

1 Tips & tricks

1
Funnel = entry point into Replay, not the replay list. Strongest pattern across 5+ customers. Funnel acts as a selection filter, since the raw replay feed is too noisy past a few hundred sessions/day.
2
Break funnels down by something you don't have a hypothesis for. One PM found her product had two different customer verticals only after breaking down trial→paid. Don't just slice by browser/country.
3
Validate UI changes with a funnel before you A/B test. Cheap pre-experiment validation, much faster than waiting for a test to power. Saved one team from rolling a regressive age-verification step.
4
Funnel position is a prioritization primitive. Errors on a funnel step matter ~10× more than errors elsewhere. Same logic for alerts, surveys, NPS prompts. Reuse via funnel-actor cohorts.
5
Tune the mechanics. Three settings first-timers leave default. Strict vs. unordered · Conversion window (set to your real cycle, not 14 days) · Exclusion steps to filter exit paths.

🎨 AI creative-tools PM

2 hidden audiences
Built trial→paid funnel as her main dashboard. Breaking it down revealed her product had two different verticals (B2C creators vs. event organizers) converting at very different rates.
Funnel as discovery tool, not just a KPI.

🇪🇺 EdTech (interview prep)

daily ritual
Engineers run a daily replay-watching ritual scoped to four named flows. One funnel per flow → click highest drop-off → watch failed sessions. Shipped dozens of small UI fixes from this loop.
A daily funnel-driven replay ritual finds friction no monitoring tool would alert on.

🎓 Education non-profit

saved weeks
Suspected an age-verification step would tank conversion. Shipped to a slice → watched the funnel → saw the drop → replays showed where users got confused → redesigned before wide rollout.
Funnel + replay loop is the cheapest insurance against shipping bad UX.
