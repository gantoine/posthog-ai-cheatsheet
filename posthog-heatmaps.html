Heatmaps Cheatsheet

Free, near-zero perf cost for clicks, scrolls, and movement, all next to your analytics.

Why use it

Free, near-zero perf cost click/scroll/movement overlays. Data piggybacks on autocapture events you already capture, with no separate SDK, no consent banner, no billing line. The differentiator: heatmap data lives next to your analytics, so you can pivot from click cluster → funnel → replay → experiment.

Most common mistake: open the Heatmap and stop. Open all three views.

1 Three views, not one

HeatmapPosition-basedClicks/movements by pixel. Clicks don't need a clickable element. See where users tried to click on dead pixels. Open for discovery.
ClickmapElement-basedAutocapture badge on each clickable element with total + rage-click counts. Resilient to layout shifts. Open for direct comparison.
ScrollmapDepth gradientRequires $pageleave events. Is the hero CTA above the fold? Does anyone reach the FAQ? Open for reach.

2 Tips & tricks

1
Use the Toolbar, not the in-app Heatmaps scene. Toolbar overlays heatmaps on the live site. Works on auth-gated pages, catches ~10× more issues, lets you create actions from elements without leaving the page.
2
Pair heatmaps with rage-click-filtered Replays. $rageclick + feature_flag = new-checkout = debugging a release in two clicks. Jump from Replay timestamp straight to the aggregate heatmap.
3
Wildcard URLs for templated pages. /products/*, /posts/*, /users/:id. Difference between “12 clicks, meaningless” and “12,000 clicks, here's what actually happens.”
4
Scroll depth is a property on every pageview. Use $prev_pageview_max_scroll_percentage as a filter/metric anywhere: funnels, experiments. “Reached 80% before clicking CTA.”
5
Autocapture clicks ARE the quantitative heatmap signal. Query click counts in HogQL, use them as experiment goals, hand them to an agent via MCP. Same data, three surfaces.
